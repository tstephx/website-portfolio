Featured Projects

Contract Transfer Process Scaling

Sole Process Owner | 680% Volume Growth | Zero Additional Headcount

Challenge: Amazon's DSP ownership transfer process was manual, ad-hoc, and had a 15-case backlog. Annual volume was about to explode from 5 cases to 63+ with no additional staff, no documentation, and no data infrastructure.

Action: I redesigned the entire workflow into a structured 14-week, 21-milestone framework. Built SQL-based analytics on Amazon Redshift tracking 689 data attributes across 209 candidates. Authored the CT Process Playbook, created 19 standardized communication templates, and developed a defect tracking system with 5-Why root cause analysis.

Result: Scaled the program 680% single-handedly while maintaining 80–100% post-transfer quality. Projected $25M in annual savings by 2026 through exit cost optimization. Reframed the function from “customer service” to “network protection,” changing how leadership valued and resourced the program.

Pinnacle BPR Scoring Methodology Redesign

Root Cause Analysis | 198 Interview Records | Mathematical Proof

Challenge: Amazon's gateway interview for DSP expansion saw approval rates collapse from 86% to 56%. No one could explain why qualified candidates were being rejected at unprecedented rates.

Action: I reverse-engineered the scoring formula from 198 interview records and proved it contained a mathematical flaw — a single dissenting panelist could override two supporters. Designed a hybrid replacement methodology (60% Median + 25% Vote Weight + 15% Deliverables Quality), created 33 behaviorally anchored rubric items across four categories, and built calibration protocols for panelist training.

Result: Eliminated the single-dissent veto problem entirely. Reduced extreme scoring variance from 77% of panels to a target of under 15-point average gaps — a 63%+ reduction. Restored the approval pathway to a 65–70% target rate, unblocking a critical growth pipeline.

DSP Application Redesign

Product Strategy | 17 Countries | $2.1M+ Entitlement Capture

Challenge: Amazon's delivery partner application hadn't been updated since 2018. It had a 74% abandonment rate, took 3+ hours to complete, and only 23% of approved candidates converted to program inclination — leaving $2.1M–$2.35M in annual entitlement on the table.

Action: I benchmarked against Chick-fil-A's franchise application (152 questions vs. our 71, yet higher completion), authored a BRD with 50+ user stories, and designed a modular multi-stage architecture supporting 6 acquisition channels across 17 countries. Replaced 20 complex questions with single-response formats and proposed a Pardot integration that cut the development timeline from 10–12 months to 3–4 months.

Result: Projected completion rate increase from 26% to 60% (+130%), program inclination from 23% to 46% (+100%), and 6,087 additional approved applications annually. Reduced time per question from 3.51 minutes to 1.5 minutes.

Pinnacle Selection Automation

SQL Architecture | Haversine Formula | From Crashing Excel to Real-Time

Challenge: The Pinnacle eligibility process ran on a 55+ MB Excel file with index-match calculations across 24,000 rows that regularly crashed. It used inconsistent traffic-dependent distance calculations, had quarterly data lag, and caused Pinnacle launches to drop 55% (93 to 42) while 20 eligible candidates were bypassed entirely.

Action: I diagnosed root causes across data, process, and tooling dimensions. Designed and built a 6-step SQL query architecture on Amazon Redshift, implemented the Haversine distance formula for consistent geospatial calculations, created a multi-source data integration pipeline, and built an outreach prioritization ranking algorithm connected to Salesforce.

Result: Pinnacle fill rate jumped from 57.9% to 94.1% (+36 percentage points), with July hitting 100%. Preserved 20 eligible candidates from being bypassed. Replaced a fragile manual process with an automated, real-time system.
